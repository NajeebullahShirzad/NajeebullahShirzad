Canada’s Slowing Economy — Data-Driven Infographic (2025)


Project Overview

This project is a visually–driven infographic that explains the key factors behind Canada’s economic slowdown in 2025. Using official economic indicators and multiple trusted sources, it translates complex data, such as GDP growth, export trends, job impacts, and monetary policy changes into clear, engaging graphics that can be easily understood by a general audience.

View Full Infographic on Canva

Project Goals

  • Why Canada’s economy is slowing down
  • What indicators show this slowdown
  • Who is being affected
  • What policy responses are underway
  • What experts expect to happen next

Key Contributions

  • GDP Growth Visualization: A clean line chart showing changes in GDP growth from 2024–2025.
  • Cost of Living Snapshot: A simple breakdown of the average monthly cost of living in Canada.
  • Export Decline Insight: A concise explanation of how global demand and tariffs have reduced Canadian exports by ~4%.
  • Employment Overview: Highlights which groups and industries are most affected by rising job pressures.
  • Monetary Policy Update: Explains the Bank of Canada’s interest rate cuts (5.0% → 4.25%) and why this matters for households and businesses.
  • Forward-Looking Summary: A short section on what’s expected going into 2026.

What I Did

  • Researched data from Statistics Canada, Bank of Canada, OECD, and Numbeo.
  • Synthesized long economic reports into short, audience-friendly insights.
  • Designed the infographic layout, text hierarchy, icons, and visual flow.
  • Created clear data visualizations while keeping the design minimal and readable.
  • Ensured the final design works for both digital and print formats.
