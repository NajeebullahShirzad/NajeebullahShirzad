AI Impact on Employment in Durham Region/ GTA


Project Overview

This team project explores how the rise of artificial intelligence is reshaping job stability, retraining needs, and affordability for middle-aged workers in Durham Region and the Greater Toronto Area. Using labour-market data, AI-exposure research, and regional employment statistics, the project analyzes which occupations face the highest automation risk and how financial pressures, such as debt loads, rising costs of living, and retraining expenses affect workers aged 35–55. Through data visualizations, personas, and storytelling, the project presents the real-world challenges faced by displaced workers like John and Jane while highlighting potential policy solutions, upskilling pathways, and regional training supports that can help residents adapt in an AI-driven economy.


AI & The future of work: Durham Region (Video Presentation)



Key Contributions

  • Analysis: Examined AI exposure datasets from Statistics Canada, Durham Workforce Authority, and provincial labour-market tools.
  • Visualization: Designed interactive visuals (maps, bar chart races, skill maps, sector breakdowns) using Flourish and Excel.
  • Narrative Design: Created personas and scripts to translate abstract labour-market trends into relatable human stories.
  • Research Synthesis: Integrated policy reports, academic research, and government datasets to build evidence-based insights.
  • Video Production: Constructed a scrollytelling video combining voiceover, visuals, and storytelling to communicate findings clearly.

Why This Project Matters

This project showcases the real impact of AI on working Canadians, especially those at the highest risk of displacement. It demonstrates the importance of accessible retraining, targeted policy solutions, and data-informed decision-making for creating an inclusive and resilient workforce.
