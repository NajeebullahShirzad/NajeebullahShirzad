Housing Affordability in Toronto, Scrollytelling Data Story


Project Overview

his project explores the growing housing affordability crisis in Toronto, with a specific focus on first-time home buyers. Using real data from Statistics Canada, National Bank, CMHC, and Ratehub, the goal was to transform a complex social and economic issue into a clear, engaging, and data-driven visual narrative.
The project evolved through three iterations: brainstorming, early visual drafting, and finally a full scrollytelling experience designed in Canva. The final iteration is the core highlight and is the version showcased in this portfolio.




Project Goals

  • Explain why housing affordability has worsened in Toronto
  • Show how this impacts young and first-time home buyers
  • Identify barriers (income, savings, stress test, mortgage rates) preventing homeownership
  • Suggest practical strategies that might help new buyers

Final Scrollytelling Narrative

The last iteration is a polished, narrative-driven scrollytelling piece built in Canva. It combines data visualizations, story flow, and human-centered messaging to engage viewers.

View the Storytelling on Canva

Key Features of the Final Version

  • Scrollytelling format: Information unfolds gradually as the viewer scrolls, improving engagement and comprehension.
  • Clear narrative arc: Introduces the problem, shows historical data trends, highlights affordability barriers, and proposes possible solutions.
  • Human-centered storytelling: Follows the experience of a typical first-time buyer household trying to enter the Toronto housing market.
  • Clean visuals and consistent design: Uses accessible icons, charts, and infographics for clarity.
  • Data-supported insights: Incorporates actual market data to ground the story in evidence.

What the Final Slides Convey

  • Introduction to the Affordability Problem: Sets the stage with inflation, housing demand, supply constraints, and rising construction costs.
  • Profile of First-Time Buyers: Explains how the typical buyer’s age and income have shifted over the years, making homeownership more difficult.
  • Historical Price Trends: Shows how housing prices have outpaced income growth.
  • Mortgage Barrier Breakdown: Visualizes down payment requirements, stress test challenges, and borrowing costs.
  • Coping Strategies Used by Buyers: Includes parental support, buying outside Toronto, shifting to smaller units, or renting longer.
  • Practical Recommendations & Advice: Provides actionable insights for new buyers navigating the current market.

Audience

Primary: Young individuals or couples preparing for their first home purchase in Toronto

Secondary: Policymakers and housing authorities seeking insights into buyer challenges


Tools & Methods

Scrollytelling as the main storytelling technique

Canva for layout, design, and interaction flow

Miro for brainstorming and early storyboarding

Data sources: Statistics Canada, CMHC, National Bank, Ratehub


Reflection

The final scrollytelling format allowed me to build a visually compelling and structured narrative. It highlighted how design, storytelling, and data visualization can work together to reveal a complex social issue in a format that’s easy for audiences to understand and emotionally connect with. The biggest learning was understanding how iteration improves clarity—each version clarified the message until the final narrative felt intuitive and complete.
